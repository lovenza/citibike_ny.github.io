NYC Citi Bike Ridership Patterns in Response to Daily Rhythms and Weather

Analyzing 100,000 rides from September 2025


üìä Project Overview

Citi Bike has become an integral part of New York City‚Äôs public transportation network, serving millions of rides. As a data-rich system, it offers a unique opportunity to understand the mobility patterns of a major metropolis.

The goal of this project is to analyze how NYC‚Äôs population uses the Citi Bike system, focusing on key external factors and usage patterns. Through comprehensive statistical analysis and interactive visualizations, we explore the intricate relationship between urban mobility, temporal patterns, and environmental conditions.

‚ùì Core Research Questions

We will address the following key questions using our 100,000-ride sample from September 2025:

1. Temporal Patterns

How do predictable daily and weekly rhythms (e.g., time of day, weekday vs.¬†weekend) shape ridership? We investigate commuter peaks, weekend leisure patterns, and the daily pulse of urban mobility.

2. User Segmentation

How do different user groups (members vs.¬†casual riders) use the system differently in terms of trip volume and duration? Understanding these patterns helps inform system optimization and user experience improvements.

3. Weather Quantification

To what extent does daily weather (Max Temperature, Precipitation) influence the decision to ride a bike and the duration of the trip? We quantify the specific impact of weather variables on ridership behavior.

üõ†Ô∏è Project Structure and Analysis Pipeline

The website is organized to follow our analytical workflow. Each section builds upon the previous, creating a comprehensive narrative of our findings:

1. Data Wrangling
Details our data collection, the stratified sampling strategy creating the 100k-ride dataset, cleaning, and merging trip-level data with daily NOAA weather data. This foundation ensures robust and representative analysis.

2. EDA & Visualization
Presents a comprehensive exploratory analysis of temporal (hourly, daily) and user-type patterns, including plots demonstrating the relationship between ridership and key weather variables. Visual insights reveal hidden patterns in urban mobility.

3. Statistical Analysis
Quantifies the specific impact of weather (e.g., effect per 10¬∞F or 1-inch of precipitation) and day-of-week on individual ride duration using multivariate linear regression models. Evidence-based conclusions drive our recommendations.

4. Interactive Dashboard
Explore the data yourself! Our Shiny dashboard allows you to filter by user type, time period, and weather conditions. Visualize patterns in real-time and discover insights through interactive exploration.
